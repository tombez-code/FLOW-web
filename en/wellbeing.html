Wellbeing and Personal/Social Education

We create calm, predictable classrooms where every child feels known and supported in their learning and growth.

Socioemotional development is woven into every part of our day. We create spaces where children feel seen, heard, and valued. Through daily routines, reflection, and caring relationships, children develop the emotional intelligence and social skills they need to thrive. We teach them to understand themselves and others, to make good choices, and to contribute kindly to their community.

Daily Routines That Make It Visible

  • Morning Circle: Connecting as a community, sharing feelings, setting intentions.
  • Quiet Work Time: Focused, distraction-free learning with low-stress environments.
  • Community Responsibility: Rotating jobs that help the class function, fostering belonging and ownership.
  • Reflection Time: Children pause to think about their choices, feelings, and growth.
  • Closing Circle: Celebrating successes, acknowledging challenges, building togetherness.

What Children Learn

Social and Emotional Learning (SEL) Strands

  • Self-Awareness: Recognising and naming their own feelings, strengths, and areas for growth.
  • Self-Management: Developing strategies to calm down, manage frustration, and make good choices.
  • Social Awareness: Understanding others' perspectives, practicing empathy, and recognising when someone needs help.
  • Relationship Skills: Cooperating, communicating clearly, handling conflict with respect, and building friendships.
  • Responsible Decision-Making: Thinking through consequences, considering others' wellbeing, and making ethical choices.

Prevention and School Culture

  • Peer Mediation: Older students learn to help resolve conflicts, supporting a culture of respect.
  • Bullying Prevention: Clear expectations and active response ensure every child feels safe.
  • Mental Health Awareness: We talk openly about emotions and stress, normalising mental wellness.
  • Inclusive Community: Celebrating diversity and ensuring every child knows they belong.

We teach children to express what they see and feel, to name a problem, ask for help, and alert adults to situations that aren't okay. Openness and safety go hand in hand at FLOW.

External specialists: We also invite trusted external educators to deepen themes of healthy relationships, risk prevention, and anti-bullying/cyberbullying work. They complement our daily classroom work and offer children and families fresh perspectives.

How Support Works

  • Observation: Teachers notice when a child is struggling emotionally or socially.
  • One-to-One Conversation: A quiet check-in helps the child feel heard and understood.
  • Family Connection: We partner with parents to understand what's happening at home and school.
  • Skill-Building: We teach concrete strategies—breathing exercises, problem-solving steps, communication techniques.
  • Specialist Support: When needed, we connect families with school psychologists or counsellors.

Support builds on what children already know from daily routines and practiced calming strategies. This makes help feel natural and understandable.

For Families

Regular updates about your child's emotional growth and social interactions, not just academics.

Resources and ideas for supporting SEL at home — books, conversations, activities that build emotional skills.

Open dialogue about any concerns, with a shared commitment to supporting your child's whole wellbeing.

When children feel emotionally safe and supported, they learn better, think more clearly, and grow into more resilient, compassionate people. That's the heart of our approach to wellbeing.
